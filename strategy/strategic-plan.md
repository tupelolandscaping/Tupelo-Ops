**Arlington, VA Landscaping Business (Tupelo Landscaping LLC)**

# 1. Executive Summary

**Current position.** The business is an Arlington, VA landscaping LLC operating at roughly a $40,000 annualized revenue run-rate in 2026 (up from ~$17,000 the prior year), with approximately six recurring clients plus project work. Calibrated against Jan–Jun 2026 Homeworks actuals, revenue is currently **~58% project work and ~42% maintenance** (largely two commercial accounts; residential recurring mowing is ~5% — the growth engine starts near zero). The business runs on one truck, a crew of ~4 reliable workers (high-school and gap-year), flat-rate pricing set from internal hourly estimates, and owner-led door-to-door acquisition. Cash on hand is **$1,225.33 as of 2026-07-06** (verified ACTUAL across all four active Relay accounts; see Section 9 Assumptions Log and `HISTORY.md` H-039) against **~$5,600** in deferred, interest-free obligations — Xavier's ~$1,800 payout (`HISTORY.md` H-021) plus the owner's $3,800 truck-debt repayment (`HISTORY.md` H-041) — and financial tracking has been informal (Homeworks adoption began ~March 2026). The owner relocates out of state for college in January 2027 and returns each summer.

**Objective.** The long-range ambition is ~$20,000/month, which the analysis establishes as a 2028-or-later horizon rather than a within-year target. Within this plan window (July 2026 – June 2027), the realistic and load-bearing objectives are twofold: roughly double peak monthly revenue toward ~$10,000 (anchored on the actual May 2026 peak of $4,908), and — more importantly — rebuild the business so it operates and grows without the owner physically present.

**Core strategic logic.** The binding constraint is labor density and owner-dependence, not demand alone. The plan rests on three convergent moves. First, make **mowing the systematized, delegable, higher-margin growth engine** (it quotes, schedules, and trains more cleanly than project work), while holding project work as a reactive baseline served on demand. Second, build **owner-independent operations** before December — a remote-owner / local-crew-lead structure, Homeworks systematization, and a recruited weekday crew lead with redundancy. Third, capture the **spring 2027 recurring-mowing window** through fall pre-sells and acquisition channels that run without the owner on the ground.

**Governing disciplines.** Two principles run through every section and hedge the majority of the risk register: never get ahead of proven demand or capacity, and systematize so the business rests on roles and processes rather than irreplaceable people.

# 3. Sequencing Map

The plan succeeds or fails on order. The decisions below are **load-bearing** — they gate everything downstream. Listed in priority order, with the reason each must come when it does.

- **Secure a redundant crew lead.** The single most time-sensitive decision. Autonomous operation requires months of supervised reps before December, so the lead must be identified early. The Konji conversation is underway (decision targeted ~July 7); recruit/cross-train a backup in parallel, beginning on that decision — do not assume one uncommitted person.

- **Build the $4,000 reserve, then the $2,000 truck hedge, before scaling.** Scaling below the reserve makes a single failure event (a slow week plus a transmission repair) potentially insolvent. The reserve is reached ~September; the truck hedge has priority claim on the next accumulation toward the $6,000 buffer. Hiring and aggressive commitment wait until the reserve is in place.

- **Systematize via Homeworks before December.** Rate matrix pricing, saved routes, custom visit forms, and job costing both enable remote operation and form the fallback if the lead search fails — they lower the skill bar of the role.

- **Start lead capture now.** Every un-recorded door-knock is a permanently lost spring prospect. The cost of starting is near zero; the cost of delay compounds.

- **Run the fall pre-sell push.** Fall is the window of highest owner control and lowest competition. Pre-selling commitments then compensates for the owner’s physical absence during the spring decision window.

- **Change the registered agent before the move — completed (June 2026).** Retained here as a record; the compliance lapse it guarded against is resolved.

Everything else — the spring campaign, cohort onboarding, and equipment reinvestment — is **demand-triggered** and sequenced after these load-bearing items.

# 4. Labor & Org Plan

The target structure separates the owner’s remotely-performable functions (strategy, quoting, scheduling, finances, client escalation) from the functions requiring local presence (driving, on-site crew leadership, equipment handling). The crew lead is the local executor of a system the owner runs remotely — a narrower, more findable role than a general manager.

- All workers remain W-2 employees through the payroll provider, with workers’ compensation in force — avoid 1099 misclassification.

# 5. Marketing & Acquisition Calendar

The strategy replaces owner-led door-to-door (which cannot survive the owner’s departure) with a free, compounding, owner-independent foundation, accelerated by the spring campaign. Acquisition economics support this shift: with a lifetime profit of ~$890 per recurring mowing client, an acquisition cost up to ~$300 is justified, well above the cost of any channel below.

## Q3 2026 (now) — Build the compounding foundation

- Complete Google Business Profile; turn on automated review requests; ask current clients for reviews (reviews take months to build — start now).

- Begin recording and tagging every door-knock lead in Homeworks for spring re-outreach.

- Stand up the referral program (give-get, ~$25–50/side, via Homeworks customer credits).

## Q4 2026 — Pre-sell while present; build assets

- Fall pre-sell push: sign spring 2027 mowing agreements (locked rates, no prepay) while competition is low and the owner can canvass.

- Design and order yard signs and truck branding (lead time); deploy signs on serviced lawns.

- Draft and load the February spring campaign (SMS/email) into Homeworks automations, date-triggered.

## Q1 2027 — Fire the spring campaign (owner remote)

- February: automated re-outreach to captured leads (interested-spring and has-service-declined buckets) — runs remotely.

- Feb–Mar: Anais weekend canvassing with a branded door-hanger; activate Google Local Services Ads / Nextdoor as paid accelerant once the free funnel is converting.

- Onboard the new mowing cohort in controlled waves to protect quality.

## Q2 2027 — Sustain and measure

- Referral engine and review base compound passively; owner returns mid-May to lead peak.

- Review CAC, conversion, and retention in Homeworks; refine channel mix for the next cycle.
